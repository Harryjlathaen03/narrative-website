Landmarks

View historical architecture of The North East

Explore the North East's hidden gems

When on your visit, make sure you take a look at the fantastic historical landmarks of The North East

Durham Cathedral

Durham Cathedral, known as the 'Cathedral of Christ; was built in 1133. It is a monumental landmark of the North East.

Bamburgh Castle

Bamburgh Castle, built in the 11th century Is one of the most historical landmarks to visit when in the North East

Angel of the north

The Angel of the North, built in 1944 by Antony Gormley: represents the coal minors who worked beneath the angel for 200 years in the dark.

Beamish

"Beamish Is a world famous open air museum which brings the history of the North East to life at It's 1820's"

Alnwick Castle

Alnwick castle, built in the 11th century Is a monumental landmark of the North East. Harry Potter and Downton abby were filmed there making It a popular place to visit.

Cragside

Cragside is a National trust site near a small town in Rothbury. It's a great place to enjoy the countryside with over 40 miles worth of footpaths.
